Advice/Definitions

RSU's

RSU stands for Restricted Stock Units. They are a promise of future stock, meaning they cannot be used immediately, instead they are vested when reaching certain milestones. When vested the employee receives the stocks. (Ex. Every 6 months for the next two years)

Stock Option

Stock options are another form of compensation given to employees by the employer. They give the employee the right to purchase a certain amount of company's shares for a set price, this price is often discounted but not always. They usually aren't all available right away and are given out over a set period of time agreed upon in the job offer.

401(K)

A retirement savings plan offered by the employer, where employees have the option to contribute a percentage of their salary into a retirement account (before taxes). Companies may offer a "match" which means they will match your contribution, usually up to a certain percentage, to incentivize savings.

Pension

A benefit plan given to employees where they receive a regular income from the employer after they retire. This amount can be based on various factors, including: time with the company, salary history, accrual rate, etc.

Parental Leave

Parental Leave is a benefit for employees available from most, but not all employers. This is a paid or unpaid leave that is given to employees when they become parents. The amount of leave is dependent upon the company.

Severance Package

A severance package is compensation or benefits that are provided if your employment is terminated. A typical severance package may have a few weeks of salary or a continuation of benefits for a temporary time after termination. These packages can vary depending on the employer

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the result recommendations guaranteed?

This tool gives recommendations based on the data you input. While it helps you make an informed decision, this tool is not a substitute for professional advice.

What is the purpose of this tool?

This tool helps you compare two job offers against each other to make a more informed decision on which to take. It takes factors such as salary, stock options, growth, and more into account!

How do I use this tool?

To use this tool please just input the required information and your preferences then hit compare. You will then be prompted to go to the results page to see the outcome of the comparison.

Can I compare more than two job offers?

Currently, you can only compare two job offers at a time. Hopefully, in the near future we will update our application to support multi-job offer comparisons.

How does this tool calculate the job rankings?

This tool compares the factors against each other, (e.g., comparing salary of both jobs), and gives the winning job a "point". Additional points are also given to jobs that have a winning category that matches the user's preferences.
